Over the last 30 years, the majority of revolutions in Biomedical Sciences have been possible due to the advancement in technology and engineering. NGS is one of many such revolutions. With applications such as personalized medicine and prescribing medicines according to the genome of an individual, along with further simplifying this task to a level where individual genomes could be drawn up within a few hours, NGS might actually turn out to be the most relevant and important revolution of the 21st century. Personalized medicine brings together data scientists, doctors, and biomedical scientists. In this course, students will be introduced to the basics and background of sequencing. They will also be taught to build Machine learning-based models for cancer and other disease based upon transcriptome data. The course also focuses on multi-omics analysis of the data. At the end of the course, students will be able to write their own codes in R to analyze transcriptomic data, biomarker identification, and multi-omics analysis.
The only prerequisite for the course is the basic fundamentals of molecular biology and coding in R and python.
